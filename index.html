Piezo Protection Cap

CATIA V6 First Principles Analysis Design Automation

01. Challenge

The Next-Gen Vehicle program required durability testing where fasteners with built-in piezo sensors had to be shielded from mud and high-pressure water. The design faced a critical contradiction:

  • Sealing: Must be water-tight to protect the sensor.
  • Usability: Must be hand-pressible for technicians to install/remove quickly without tools.
  • Impact Resistance: Technicians often strike caps with a mallet to ensure a secure fit. The design had to withstand this blunt impact without cracking.
  • Scale: Must fit 10+ different fastener variants across 20+ joints.

02. Action

Design & Calculation: I created a hand-pressibility calculator using Lamé’s equations (P) and friction formulas (F = μPA) to determine the exact interference fit required.

Validation: Tested prototypes in CATIA and physically with 3 materials (ASA, TPU, Nylon). Selected ASA as the only material that passed impact, water submersion, and hand-pressibility tests.

Automation: Developed a "Powercopy" VBA automation tool for CATIA. This allowed engineers to simply select a hex head type and dimensions to instantly generate a manufacturing-ready cap model.

03. Impact

  • Achieved a 100% seal rate across all 20+ joint variants during endurance testing.
  • Selected ASA material as the superior choice, surviving both the water pressure and the technician's mallet strikes.

Torque Data Processor

Python Pandas GUI

01. Challenge

Torque-to-failure (TTF) testing for a specific next-gen vehicle required multiple tests to be done in-line. The original method relied on manual point picking to find peak prevailing, snug, and yield points from the data traces. This was slow and inefficient for high-volume testing.

02. Action

Algorithm Development: I built a Python-based tool that processes angle-to-torque graph traces. It automatically determines the peak prevailing, snug, and yield points based on user-defined parameters using a specific reference point scanning logic.

Reporting: Automated the workflow so that after point picking, a PDF report is immediately exported, allowing engineers to review data summaries at a glance.

Deployment: Designed the tool to be compatible with micro-control tools used in Gigafactories at Reno, Berlin, and Shanghai.

03. Impact

  • Reduced analysis time by 30+ minutes per joint.
  • Successfully deployed the tool to global teams in Reno, Berlin, and Shanghai, standardizing the TTF reporting process.

ReAX Transport Fixtures

PTC Creo GD&T 3D Printing

01. Challenge

Risk: Critical connector joints on ReAX steering systems were sustaining damage during international shipments to Germany and China.

Requirement: Needed a custom fixture that could protect the exposed connectors from impact and vibration while fitting within standard shipping containers.

02. Action

Design: I designed three unique protective fixtures in PTC Creo tailored to the ReAX geometry. I utilized GD&T to ensure the internal clearances were tight enough to prevent movement but loose enough for easy manual installation.

Manufacturing: 3D printed prototypes using FDM to verify fit-up on the physical units before approving the final shipment configuration.

03. Impact

  • Ensured 100% successful delivery of prototype units to international facilities.
  • Prevented project delays caused by shipping damage.

Formula SAE Aerodynamics

SolidWorks ANSYS CFD Composites

01. Challenge

The racing team needed to increase cornering speeds without compromising straight-line acceleration. The existing aero package generated insufficient downforce relative to the drag penalty.

02. Action

Simulation: I modeled multi-element front and rear wings in SolidWorks and performed iterative CFD simulations in ANSYS Fluent to optimize the angle of attack and flap geometry.

Fabrication: Engineered the physical wing structure using a carbon fiber skin over foam cores with machined aluminum mounting points for rigidity.

03. Impact

  • Optimized the lift-to-drag ratio, achieving a 30% drag reduction while maintaining target downforce.
  • Established a standardized CFD workflow for future aerodynamic sub-teams.

Pipsqueak Stirling Engine

Siemens NX Machining GD&T

01. Challenge

To design and manufacture a fully functional thermal-to-mechanical energy conversion system (Stirling Engine) from scratch. The main difficulty was achieving the extremely tight air-tight tolerances required for the piston cylinder without industrial-grade precision equipment.

02. Action

Modeling: I modeled 18 distinct components in Siemens NX, applying strict GD&T controls to critical mating surfaces.

Materials & Assembly: I utilized a combination of brass, nylon, and polished metal to manage friction and thermal expansion. I spent over 260 hours machining and assembling the components into a custom "Pirate Ship" form factor.

03. Impact

  • Achieved a fully functional engine that runs continuously on a simple heat differential.
  • Demonstrated mastery of complex assembly modeling and manual machining tolerances.

Siemens NX Class-A Surfacing Reverse Engineering
